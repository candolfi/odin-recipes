Slow Cooked Pork Ribs

Slow cooked pork ribs finished on the Barbecue

Perfect for a summer barbecue, these ribs are fall off the bone good and can be paired with virtually anything.

They are sure to be a crowd pleaser and are so simple to make!

Ingredients

Serves 3-4
  • 1-2 kg side of ribs (the packages vary greatly in weight so as much as you like really)
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 2 tsp ground black pepper
  • 2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper
  • Enough of your favourite barbecue sauce to cover the ribs

Steps

  1. Start off by pre-heating the oven to 250 degrees Fahrenheit
  2. Prepare the ribs by removing the membrane if not done by the butcher already
  3. Mix all the seasonings together to create a dry spice rub
  4. Generously season the ribs on all sides with the spice rib
  5. Place the ribs in a deep dish. If they don't fit, it's ok to cut them up into smaller portions
  6. Cover the dish with tin foil (It is very important to seal the dish well. Otherwise the ribs will dry out!)
  7. Cook the ribs for atleast 2 hours, preferably 3
    8. At this point you have the option of finishing the ribs with the oven broiler or a barbecue
  8. Remove the ribs and brush on your barbecue sauce before placing them uncovered in the broiler or on the barbecue at high heat for 3-4 minutes
  9. At this point they are ready to be enjoyed!

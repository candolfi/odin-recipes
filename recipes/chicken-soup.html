Chicken Soup

A hearty soup for those winter days

This soup is extremely versatile and easy to cook! The vegetables and spices can easily be substituted for others but I am sharing my favourite version with you today

For cold weather or simply days where you need a pick me up, this is the perfect recipe

Ingredients

Serves 3-4
  • 1 medium yellow onion (diced)
  • 2 medium sized carrots (chopped coarsely)
  • 2 ribs of celery (chopped coarsely)
  • 1 lb of skinless chicken (I use legs but you can use whatever you like!)
  • 1/4 cup of red lentils
  • salt, pepper, oregano and red chili flakes to taste
  • A dash of olive oil

Steps

  1. First off, start my turning the kettle on. You'll need enough water to cover your soup ingredients
  2. Add olive oil to a large pot on medium heat
  3. Once the oil begins to shimmer, add all of your vegetables and fry until the onions and celery become translucent
  4. Next add the boiling water, followed by the chicken. Afterwards, top up with additional water to cover the ingredients as necessarry
  5. Add your seasoning (as much or as little as you like)
  6. Get the soup up to a boil and then cover and simmer for 90 - 120 minutes with a lid on
  7. This part is optional but at this point, I like to remove the chicken and take the meat off the bones before re-adding it to the soup
  8. Lastly, add your red lentils and cook for a final 10 minutes before serving!
